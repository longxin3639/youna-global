Decision Guide

Air Freight vs Sea Freight: Which Is Right for You?

Choose Air Freight When:

  • Delivery needed within 10 days
  • Shipment value is high per kg
  • Goods are time-sensitive (seasonal, launch date)
  • Total shipment weight is under 500kg
  • Product is lightweight but bulky (electronics, fashion)
  • You need to replenish FBA inventory fast

Choose Sea Freight When:

  • Cost per unit is the top priority
  • Shipment exceeds 500kg or fills a container
  • You have 3-5 weeks lead time
  • Products are heavy or dense (furniture, hardware)
  • You're importing regular bulk orders
  • Shipping cost significantly impacts your margins
Pro Tip: Many experienced importers use a hybrid strategy — sea freight for regular bulk orders and air freight for urgent replenishments or new product launches. We can set up both shipping channels for you simultaneously.
FAQ

Common Questions About Shipping from China

How much does it cost to ship from China?

It depends on shipping method, weight, volume, and destination. Express courier costs $5-15/kg for small parcels. Air freight costs $4-8/kg for 100kg+. Sea freight costs $1,500-4,000 per 20ft container depending on destination and season. Contact us with your shipment details for an exact quote.

How long does shipping from China take?

Express courier (DHL/FedEx/UPS): 3-7 days. Air freight: 5-10 days. Sea freight FCL: 15-35 days depending on destination. Sea freight LCL: 20-40 days. Rail freight to Europe: 15-20 days. We provide estimated delivery dates for every quote.

What is the cheapest way to ship from China?

Sea freight is always the cheapest for large shipments. A 20ft container from Shenzhen to Los Angeles costs around $1,500-3,000. For parcels under 5kg, ePacket or China Post Ordinary Small Packet is cheapest but slowest (15-40 days). For medium shipments (100-500kg), LCL sea freight or consolidated air freight offers the best value.

Should I use air freight or sea freight from China?

Use air freight when shipment is urgent (under 10 days), cargo is high-value but lightweight, or goods are perishable. Use sea freight when shipment is large (over 100kg), cost per unit matters more than speed, and you have 3-5 weeks lead time. Many importers use both — sea for bulk orders, air for urgent restocks.

Do you handle customs clearance on both sides?

We handle Chinese export customs declaration, including commodity inspection and certificate of origin. For import customs in your destination country, our shipping partners can arrange DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) service which covers everything, or we can guide your customs broker with the correct documentation.

Can I combine goods from multiple suppliers into one shipment?

Absolutely — this is one of our most popular services. Goods from different suppliers arrive at our Guangdong warehouse, where we consolidate everything into one shipment. This saves you significantly on shipping costs compared to shipping each order separately. You get 15-20 days free warehouse storage.
