01 Research & Define Your Product

Before you start looking for suppliers, you need to clearly define what you want to import:

  • Product specifications: Size, material, color, features, packaging requirements
  • Target market: Who will buy this product? What is their budget?
  • Quantity: How many units do you need for your first order?
  • Competition: Research similar products on Amazon, eBay, or local markets
Pro Tip: Start with 2-3 product variations instead of a large catalog. Test the market before scaling up.

02 Find Suppliers in China

There are several ways to find Chinese suppliers:

Online Platforms

  • Alibaba: Largest B2B marketplace, good for international buyers
  • 1688.com: Domestic Chinese platform with lower prices (requires Chinese language)
  • Global Sources: Premium suppliers, verified exhibitors from trade shows
  • Made-in-China: Alternative to Alibaba, many factories listed

Trade Shows

  • Canton Fair: China's largest trade show (April & October in Guangzhou)
  • Yiwu Fair: Small commodities and consumer goods (October in Yiwu)
  • Hong Kong Electronics Fair: Electronics and tech products

03 Verify Suppliers & Factories

This is the most critical step to avoid scams and ensure quality:

Basic Verification

  • Check business license on Chinese government websites
  • Look for "Verified Supplier" badges on platforms
  • Ask for factory photos and videos
  • Search online for reviews and complaints

Deep Verification (Recommended)

  • Arrange a factory visit (in-person or video call)
  • Use third-party verification services
  • Check trade references and past customers
  • Verify bank account ownership
Warning: Never place large orders with unverified suppliers. 70% of scams involve fake factories or trading companies pretending to be manufacturers.

04 Request Samples

Always order samples before placing bulk orders:

  • Check quality: Test product functionality, materials, and finish
  • Verify specifications: Confirm size, weight, packaging
  • Take photos: Document sample condition for future reference
  • Compare with competitors: Ensure your product meets market standards

Sample costs: You'll typically pay 2-3x the wholesale price for samples, plus shipping. Most suppliers refund sample costs when you place a bulk order.

05 Negotiate Prices & Terms

Price Negotiation Tips

  • Get quotes from 3-5 suppliers for comparison
  • Ask about volume discounts for larger orders
  • Negotiate based on MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity)
  • Consider total cost: price + shipping + duties

Payment Terms

  • Flexible arrangements: Payment terms can be negotiated based on order size and relationship
  • Alibaba Trade Assurance: Money held until you receive goods
  • Letter of Credit (L/C): Bank-secured payment for large orders

06 Place Your Order

Order Details to Confirm

  • Product specifications (size, color, material, features)
  • Packaging requirements (branding, labels, barcodes)
  • Production timeline (typically 15-30 days)
  • Quality inspection requirements
  • Shipping method and destination

Production Monitoring

  • Request photos during production
  • Ask for production updates every 5-7 days
  • Consider mid-production inspection for large orders

07 Quality Control

Don't ship goods without quality inspection:

Inspection Types

  • Duplication Sample: Check a sample during production
  • Pre-Shipment Inspection (PSI): Full inspection before goods leave factory
  • Container Loading Supervision: Verify correct quantity during loading

What to Check

  • Quantity: Correct number of units
  • Quality: Functionality, defects, workmanship
  • Packaging: Proper packaging for international shipping
  • Labeling: Correct labels, barcodes, branding

08 Shipping & Customs

Choose Shipping Method

  • Air Freight: 3-7 days, expensive, good for urgent or small orders (under 100kg)
  • Sea Freight: 15-45 days, affordable, best for large orders (over 500kg)
  • Express (DHL/FedEx/UPS): 3-5 days, very expensive, samples only

Customs Clearance

  • Prepare required documents: commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading
  • Determine HS code for your product (affects duty rate)
  • Pay import duties and taxes
  • May require product certifications (CE, FDA, FCC, etc.)

09 Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Not Verifying Suppliers

Working with fake or unverified factories leads to scams, poor quality, or no delivery.

❌ Paying Too Much Upfront

Always verify product quality before releasing final payments. Consider working through verified intermediaries for added security.

❌ Skipping Samples

Bulk products often differ from photos. Always test samples before large orders.

❌ Underestimating Costs

Total cost includes product + shipping + duties + QC + warehousing. Calculate accurately.

❌ Ignoring Customs Requirements

Missing documentation or wrong HS codes cause delays and penalties.
