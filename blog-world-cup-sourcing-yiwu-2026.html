Yiwu International Trade City is divided into five districts, each specializing in different product categories. For World Cup merchandise, you'll mainly be working in:

06 Common Mistakes When Sourcing World Cup Products from China

Many first-time importers lose money on World Cup merchandise not because of bad products, but because of avoidable mistakes. Here are the most common pitfalls:

  • ❌ IP Infringement: Ordering products with FIFA's official logo, licensed team crests, or protected player likenesses without authorization. FIFA enforces its IP aggressively — customs can seize shipments.
  • ❌ Ordering Too Late: Waiting until May or June to place orders. By then, factories are at capacity and can't guarantee quality or delivery dates. Order now.
  • ❌ Skipping Quality Control: Assuming products look like the sample without inspecting the actual production run. Mass production often varies from samples — especially for printing and stitching quality.
  • ❌ Choosing Unverified Suppliers: Placing orders with suppliers you've never verified in person (or through a trusted agent). Trade show listings and platform profiles alone aren't enough.
  • ❌ Underestimating Shipping Costs: Calculating margins based on FOB price alone, without factoring in freight, duties, and import taxes. Your real landed cost can be 30–60% higher than the FOB price.
  • ❌ Ignoring Packaging Requirements: Different markets have different packaging and labeling requirements (CE mark for EU, FCC for electronics in the US, etc.). Non-compliance can result in customs holds or returns.
