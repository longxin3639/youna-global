Understanding Your Options

Private Label, White Label, or OEM — What's Right for You?

Not sure which manufacturing model fits your brand? Here's a clear breakdown of the three main approaches — and we support all of them.

🏷️

Private Label

Best for: Brand differentiation with moderate investment.

Take an existing factory product and customize it to your specifications — change materials, colors, features, packaging, and branding. You get a unique product without the cost of designing from scratch.

📦

White Label

Best for: Fastest time to market, lower budget.

Take a factory's ready-made product and simply apply your brand logo and packaging. No product modifications — just rebranding. Ideal for market testing and getting started quickly.

🏭

OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturing)

Best for: Full control over product design and quality.

Build a product entirely to your custom specifications. You provide the design, materials list, or technical drawings — the factory manufactures it exclusively for you. Highest investment, strongest brand ownership.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions About Private Label in China

What is the minimum order quantity (MOQ) for private label products in China?
MOQ varies by product category and customization level. Simple items like mugs or tote bags can have MOQs as low as 100-200 units. Electronics or complex products typically require 500-2,000+ units. We negotiate the lowest possible MOQ with each factory on your behalf — often significantly lower than their listed minimum.
How much does private labeling cost in China?
Private label costs include the base product price plus customization fees. Logo printing or embossing typically adds $0.10-1.00 per unit. Custom packaging (boxes, labels, inserts) adds $0.20-2.00 per unit depending on complexity. Mold fees for entirely new products range from $500-5,000+. We provide a detailed cost breakdown upfront so there are no surprises.
Can I put my own logo and brand on products from China?
Yes, absolutely. Chinese factories offer various branding methods including screen printing, heat transfer, embossing, debossing, laser engraving, metal badges, woven labels, and custom hang tags. We help you choose the best branding method for your product type, budget, and brand aesthetic.
How long does it take to create a private label product in China?
The typical timeline is: sample development (7-15 days), sample approval and revision (5-10 days), bulk production (15-45 days depending on quantity and complexity), quality inspection (1-3 days), shipping (5-30 days depending on method). Total: approximately 5-10 weeks from concept to delivery at your door.
What's the difference between private label, white label, and OEM?
White label means taking an existing factory product and putting your brand on it — fastest to launch, lowest cost. Private label involves modifying an existing product with your specifications (materials, colors, features, packaging) — more customization, moderate timeline and cost. OEM means building a product entirely to your custom design from scratch — highest investment but full control over the final product.
Do you help with product design if I don't have technical drawings?
Yes. Many of our clients start with just an idea, a sketch, or reference photos of products they like. We work with factories that have in-house design teams who can help develop your concept into a production-ready design. You don't need to be an engineer to create a private label product.
Can I order a sample before committing to bulk production?
Absolutely. We always recommend ordering samples first. We coordinate the sample process with the factory, photograph and video every detail for your remote review, and only proceed to bulk production once you're fully satisfied. Sample costs vary but typically range from $20-100 per item including shipping.
